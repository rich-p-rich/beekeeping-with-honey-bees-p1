Bees, Honey and the Natural World


Bees, honey and nature

Get Connected

A close connection to nature can inspire feelings of serenity, joy and better overall mental health: beekeeping can be your gateway to a more peaceful life.

Be Engaged

When you are inspecting your bees, they need your full attention: a good hobby helps you live in the moment, focus on what is in front of you and leave your day-to-day troubles behind.

Support Nature

Bees are a crucial part of the eco-system: most of the crops, fruits and vegetables we rely on are pollinated by bees.

Honey

Many people enjoy honey on their bread, in their porridge, as part of a salad dressing or just off a spoon, and fresh honey straight from the honeycomb can't be beat!


The Honey Bee

There are over 20,000 known species of bee in the world, from bumblebees to leafcutter bees to mason bees. But beekeeping involves keeping hives full of the Honey Bee, also known in scientific language by its Latin name of Apis Mellifera.


Honey bees are one of the world's most important pollinators and visit a huge range of plants and flowers, from apple trees to soyabeans and sunflowers; without them, agriculture suffers.


Honey Bees live in colonies of 20,000 to 80,000 depending on the time of the year and strength of the colony. There are three types of Honey Bees: the queen, the worker bees and the drones.
