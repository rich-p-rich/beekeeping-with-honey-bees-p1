Bees, Honey and the Natural World


Why keep bees?

Get Connected

A close connection to nature can inspire feelings of serenity, joy and overall increase your mental health: beekeeping can be your gateway to a more peaceful life.

Be Engaged

When you are inspecting your bees, they need your full attention: a good hobby helps you live in the moment, focus on what is in front of you and leave your day-to-day troubles behind.

Support Nature

Bees are a crucial part of the eco-system: most of the crops, fruits and vegetables we rely on are pollinated by bees. By supporting bees, from planting flowers to keeping them in your garden, you are helping our environment in a big way.

Honey

Many people enjoy honey on their bread, in their porridge, as part of a salad dressing or just off a spoon, and fresh honey straight from the honeycomb of your own bees can't be beat!

The Honey Bee

There are over 20,000 known species of bee in the world, from bumblebees to leafcutter bees to mason bees. But beekeeping involves keeping hives full of the Honey Bee, also known in scientific language by its Latin name of Apis Mellifera.


Honey Bees live in colonies of 20,000 to 80,000 depending on the time of the year and strength of the colony. And making honey is what they do. Over the centuries, beekeepers have worked out ways to replicate their natural habitat with man-made beehives with wood, polyester and even concrete. Given the size of the honey bee colony, their natural disposition for making honey and our ability to set up hives with which we can interact, beekeeping involves keeping honey bees rather than bumblebees or any other type.
